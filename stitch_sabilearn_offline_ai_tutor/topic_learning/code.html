Quadratic Equations

Master the fundamentals of second-degree polynomial equations. Learn to solve using factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.

download_done Available Offline

book_4 Definition

A quadratic equation is a second-order polynomial equation in a single variable x, with standard form:

ax² + bx + c = 0

Where x represents an unknown, and a, b, and c represent known numbers, where a ≠ 0.

Solving Methods

calculate

Factoring

Finding two numbers that multiply to 'ac' and add to 'b'. Best for simple equations.

functions

Quadratic Formula

The universal method using x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a. Works for every quadratic equation.

insights Understanding the Discriminant

expand_more

The part of the formula under the square root (b² - 4ac) is called the discriminant. It tells you the nature of the roots.

  • Positive (>0): Two distinct real roots. The parabola crosses the x-axis twice.
  • Zero (=0): One real root (repeated). The parabola touches the x-axis exactly once.
  • Negative (<0): No real roots (complex roots). The parabola never touches the x-axis.
