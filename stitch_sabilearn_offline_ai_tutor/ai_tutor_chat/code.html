Can you explain Newton's Third Law in simple terms?

Newton's Third Law of Motion

Simply put: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

This means that if object A exerts a force on object B, then object B will exert an equal and opposite force on object A. Forces always come in pairs!

Give examples

Here are a few everyday examples of Newton's Third Law in action:

  • sailing

    Swimming

    When you swim, you push the water backwards (action). The water pushes you forward (reaction) with equal force.

  • rocket_launch

    A Rocket Launching

    The rocket pushes exhaust gases downwards out of its engines (action). The gases push the rocket upwards (reaction).
